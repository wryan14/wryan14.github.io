INTP Deep Reading Journey: From Theory to Praxis

"The unexamined life is not worth living, but the unlived life is not worth examining." - Adapted from Socrates

Introduction

This reading journey is designed for the INTP mind - systematic, deep-diving, and seeking coherent frameworks that can be applied to life. Each track interweaves theoretical depth with practical application, recognizing that true understanding comes through both contemplation and implementation.

Why This Structure?

  • Built for Ti-Ne cognitive functions: systematic analysis followed by pattern recognition
  • Integrates theoretical frameworks with practical applications
  • Creates dialogues across disciplines and epochs
  • Challenges both analytical and intuitive thinking
  • Develops both vertical (deep) and horizontal (broad) understanding

Track 1: Foundational Epistemology and Methodology (Months 1-2)

Understanding how we know what we know, and how to think about thinking.

Primary Texts:

  • "The Metaphysics of Experience" by Arthur Whitehead
    Difficulty: Very High | Focus: Process Philosophy
  • "Personal Knowledge" by Michael Polanyi
    Difficulty: High | Focus: Tacit Knowledge
  • "How to Read a Book" by Mortimer Adler
    Difficulty: Moderate | Focus: Methodological Foundation

Practical Integration:

  • Create a personal epistemological framework document
  • Develop a systematic note-taking system
  • Begin a "knowledge synthesis" journal

Track 2: Ethics and Decision Theory (Months 3-4)

Bridging moral philosophy with practical decision-making.

Primary Texts:

  • "After Virtue" by Alasdair MacIntyre
    Difficulty: High | Focus: Moral Philosophy
  • "Rationality: From AI to Zombies" by Eliezer Yudkowsky
    Difficulty: High | Focus: Rational Decision-Making
  • "The Path of Least Resistance" by Robert Fritz
    Difficulty: Moderate | Focus: Structural Dynamics

Practical Integration:

  • Develop a personal ethical framework
  • Create decision-making protocols for different contexts
  • Start an "ethical decisions" journal

Track 3: Metaphysics and Technology (Months 5-6)

Understanding being and its relationship with modern technology.

Primary Texts:

  • "Being and Time" by Martin Heidegger
    Difficulty: Very High | Focus: Fundamental Ontology
  • "The Question Concerning Technology" by Martin Heidegger
    Difficulty: High | Focus: Technology's Essence
  • "The Technological Society" by Jacques Ellul
    Difficulty: High | Focus: Technique's Impact

Practical Integration:

  • Audit personal technology use through phenomenological lens
  • Develop a "technology philosophy" document
  • Create practical guidelines for authentic technological engagement

Track 4: Consciousness and Cognition (Months 7-8)

Understanding mind, consciousness, and their practical implications.

Primary Texts:

  • "The Phenomenology of Perception" by Maurice Merleau-Ponty
    Difficulty: Very High | Focus: Embodied Cognition
  • "The Origin of Consciousness in the Breakdown of the Bicameral Mind" by Julian Jaynes
    Difficulty: High | Focus: Consciousness Theory
  • "The Master and His Emissary" by Iain McGilchrist
    Difficulty: High | Focus: Brain Hemispheres

Practical Integration:

  • Develop practices for integrated thinking
  • Create exercises for hemispheric balance
  • Start a phenomenological observation journal

Track 5: Integration and Synthesis (Months 9-10)

Bringing together multiple frameworks for practical wisdom.

Primary Texts:

  • "Reason and Revelation" by Eric Voegelin
    Difficulty: Very High | Focus: Knowledge Integration
  • "The Act of Will" by Roberto Assagioli
    Difficulty: High | Focus: Practical Psychology
  • "Science and the Modern World" by Alfred North Whitehead
    Difficulty: High | Focus: Unified Worldview

Practical Integration:

  • Create a personal philosophy synthesis document
  • Develop practical life principles
  • Design personal practices based on integrated understanding

Track 6: Application and Communication (Months 11-12)

Learning to apply and communicate complex knowledge effectively.

Primary Texts:

  • "Philosophical Investigations" by Ludwig Wittgenstein
    Difficulty: Very High | Focus: Language Games
  • "The Rhetoric" by Aristotle
    Difficulty: High | Focus: Communication
  • "Maps of Meaning" by Jordan Peterson
    Difficulty: High | Focus: Practical Meaning

Practical Integration:

  • Develop a personal communication framework
  • Create practical guides for sharing complex ideas
  • Start teaching or writing about learned concepts

Reading Synergies

These tracks are designed to build upon each other:

  • Track 1 provides the methodological tools for all subsequent reading
  • Track 2 establishes ethical frameworks for decision-making
  • Track 3 deepens understanding of being and technology
  • Track 4 provides insights into consciousness and cognition
  • Track 5 focuses on integration and practical wisdom
  • Track 6 enables effective application and communication

Implementation Guidelines

  • Read each book twice: first for understanding, then for application
  • Maintain separate journals for theoretical insights and practical applications
  • Create concept maps connecting ideas across books and tracks
  • Schedule regular review and synthesis sessions
  • Find or create discussion groups for each major text
  • Write detailed summaries and practical applications for each book

Note: This reading plan is intensive and designed for deep engagement. Adjust the timeline and selection based on your specific interests and circumstances. The goal is transformation through understanding, not just accumulation of knowledge.
