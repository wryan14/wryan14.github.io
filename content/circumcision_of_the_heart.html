"The Spiritual Transformation of the Heart: John Wesley's Sermon, Circumcision of the Heart

Almighty God, to thee we bow, for thou hast given us hearts that feel and know; from thee thy heavenly grace doth flow, to cleanse us from our secret woe. O make our inmost soul thy care, circumcise us with thy love sincere; our stubborn wills thy grace prepare, and help us to renew our years.

Hymn Title: "Almighty God, to Thee We Bow"


In John Wesley's sermon, Circumcision of the Heart, he argues that the spiritual practices of circumcision, which are significant in Judaism, can be adapted to Christian piety. He compares physical circumcision to a spiritual alteration of the heart, emphasizing that it is not for the purpose of justification but for sanctification. He further claims that this inner transformation can only be achieved through grace and faith in Jesus Christ, as faith and grace allows for the presence of God to enter the heart of an individual. He explains that the true expression of faith does not rely on the adherence to external works alone, but requires a personal transformation of the heart. Thus, the moral law itself demands a spiritual circumcision that leads to devotion to Christ.
