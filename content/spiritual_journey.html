365-Day Journey to Digital Minimalism and Christian Mysticism

"Be still, and know that I am God." - Psalm 46:10

Introduction and Purpose

This 365-day journey is designed for a technically-oriented professional seeking to deepen their spiritual life while maintaining a balanced relationship with technology. The goal is to cultivate a rich inner life while remaining effective in the modern world, combining ancient wisdom with contemporary needs.

Why This Journey?

  • To find deeper meaning and connection with God in our digital age
  • To develop healthy boundaries with technology while leveraging its benefits
  • To create space for contemplation and spiritual growth
  • To build a sustainable practice that enhances both professional and spiritual life

Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-90)

Morning Ritual

Daily Practice:

  • Wake up at a consistent time (preferably before sunrise)
  • Burn frankincense or myrrh (traditional Christian incense)
  • 15 minutes of silent meditation
  • Brief scripture reading
  • Journal reflection (digital or analog, as preferred)

Essential Readings:

  • "Digital Minimalism" by Cal Newport
  • "The Practice of the Presence of God" by Brother Lawrence
  • "Introduction to the Devout Life" by St. Francis de Sales

Technology Guidelines:

  • Conduct a digital audit of all apps and services
  • Establish "sacred hours" free from digital interruptions
  • Create designated times for email and social media

Phase 2: Deepening (Days 91-180)

Expanded Practices:

  • 30 minutes of contemplative prayer
  • Lectio Divina practice
  • Weekly digital sabbath (24-hour period)
  • Integration of prayer beads or prayer rope

Recommended Readings:

  • "The Cloud of Unknowing" (Anonymous)
  • "New Seeds of Contemplation" by Thomas Merton
  • "The Way of the Heart" by Henri Nouwen

Phase 3: Integration (Days 181-270)

Advanced Practices:

  • Contemplative coding practices
  • Digital work-prayer integration
  • Regular silent retreats (monthly, even if brief)
  • Development of a rule of life

Philosophical Foundations:

  • "After Virtue" by Alasdair MacIntyre
  • "Technology and the Character of Contemporary Life" by Albert Borgmann
  • "The Philokalia" (selected readings)

Phase 4: Synthesis (Days 271-365)

Culminating Practices:

  • Development of personal liturgy
  • Integration of contemplative practices with technical work
  • Community engagement and sharing
  • Personal retreat planning

Advanced Readings:

  • "The Divine Names" by Pseudo-Dionysius
  • "The Interior Castle" by St. Teresa of √Åvila
  • "The Spiritual Exercises" by St. Ignatius of Loyola

Guidelines for Technical Work Integration

  • Begin each work session with a brief prayer or moment of mindfulness
  • Use AI tools mindfully and intentionally, not compulsively
  • Implement regular "tech fasts" during specific hours
  • Practice "contemplative coding" - viewing technical work as a form of meditation
  • Maintain a prayer journal (digital or physical) to track insights and growth

Monthly Review Questions

  1. How has my relationship with technology evolved this month?
  2. In what ways have I experienced God's presence in my daily work?
  3. What practices have been most meaningful?
  4. How has my technical work been enhanced or affected by these practices?
  5. What adjustments do I need to make to better integrate spiritual and professional life?
