"Circumcision of the Heart": Exploring John Wesley's Spiritual Transformation through Faith

"“Not of the flesh the law requires, Nor of external forms; A new and living way it opens, By Jesus’ blood it Comes!” "

Hymn Title: "Hymn Title: "A New and Living Way""


John Wesley's sermon, "Circumcision of the Heart," explores the concept of a spiritual circumcision for believers. Through Biblical Scriptural reference, and analogies to other aspects of the Christian faith, Wesley attempts to demonstrate the necessity of a spiritual transformation, which is signified by a heart circumcised by faith. He argues that believers must focus their efforts inward and cleave to God in order to experience the grace and love of their savior on a daily basis. Wesley argues that this deeper spiritual transformation leads to the ability to fight and resist temptations and sin, and is essential to living a righteous life.
