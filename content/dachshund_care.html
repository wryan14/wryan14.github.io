Dachshund Enrichment and Training Plan

"A well-trained dachshund is a happy dachshund, and short, consistent training sessions are more effective than long, irregular ones."

Understanding Your Long-haired Dachshund

  • Bred for hunting: high prey drive and strong nose orientation
  • Intelligent but independent-minded
  • Need for mental stimulation
  • Prone to back problems: requires special consideration
  • Social and affectionate breed

Daily Essential Routine (30-45 minutes total)

Morning Routine (10-15 minutes)

  • 5-minute brief training session before breakfast
  • Structured feeding time (use as training opportunity)
  • Quick grooming check

Exercise Requirements

  • Two 15-20 minute walks daily (morning and evening)
  • Avoid jumping and stairs when possible
  • Use ramps for furniture access

Evening Routine (10-15 minutes)

  • 5-minute training session
  • Interactive play time
  • Calm-down period before bed

Weekly Training Focus Areas

Week 1: Basic Commands

  • Sit
  • Stay
  • Come
  • Leave it

Week 2: Leash Training

  • Loose leash walking
  • Heel command
  • No pulling exercises

Week 3: Home Manners

  • Quiet command
  • Place training
  • Door manners

Week 4: Advanced Skills

  • Recall in distracting environments
  • Impulse control exercises
  • Trick training

Enrichment Activities (Choose 1-2 daily)

  • Nose work games (5-10 minutes)
    • Hide treats around the house
    • Snuffle mat feeding
    • Find it games
  • Puzzle toys during your work time
    • Kong toys
    • Lick mats
    • Interactive puzzle feeders
  • Quick training games
    • Shell game with treats
    • Target training
    • Basic agility (safe, low-impact)

Special Health Considerations

  • Maintain healthy weight to prevent back problems
  • Use ramps or steps for furniture access
  • Regular grooming for long coat
  • Monitor for signs of IVDD (Intervertebral Disk Disease)
  • Keep nails trimmed to prevent back strain

Monthly Goals

  • Master one new trick
  • Introduce one new puzzle toy
  • Practice socialization in new environments
  • Grooming session with professional

Essential Resources

Recommended Books

  • "Dachshund: A Complete Guide" by Michael Fox
  • "The Focused Puppy" by Deborah Jones
  • "Brain Games for Dogs" by Claire Arrowsmith

Useful Equipment

  • Quality harness (to prevent tracheal pressure)
  • Pet ramp or stairs
  • Snuffle mat
  • Various puzzle toys
  • Long-line leash for recall training

Online Resources

  • Dachshund Club of America (health resources)
  • Fenzi Dog Sports Academy (online training)
  • Kikopup YouTube Channel (positive training methods)

Time-Saving Tips

  • Combine training with daily activities (meals, walks)
  • Use puzzle toys during your work time
  • Set up automatic feeders for consistent timing
  • Create a rotating toy schedule to maintain interest
  • Use a grooming routine chart to stay organized

Remember: Dachshunds respond well to positive reinforcement and consistency. Even short training sessions can be highly effective when done regularly.

Signs of a Well-Adjusted Dachshund

  • Follows basic commands consistently
  • Shows appropriate energy levels
  • Interacts well with family members
  • Can settle down when needed
  • Maintains healthy play drive
  • Shows confidence in various situations
